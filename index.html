Upcoming Master Thesis – Design and implementation of a radio probe for spectrum sensing with high altitude balloons

The goal of this thesis is to continue the development of the Stratosense project.
Stratosense, which started in 2019, aims to perform spectrum sensing tasks at high altitude using helium balloons.
Spectrum sensing consists of detecting and recording radio signals across multiple frequency bands, enabling statistical analysis for each channel.
Applications include detecting and locating radio transmitters, identifying anomalous transmissions, gathering occupancy statistics, and capturing signals of potential extra-terrestrial origin.
The technical focus lies in implementing spectrum sensing algorithms on a software-defined radio probe (USRP E320 or E312).
Students may explore components such as energy- or correlation-based transmission detection, data compression, and RF signal processing.
In the second phase, real-world measurements will be collected and analyzed—both on the ground and during aerial tests using drones or helium balloons.
